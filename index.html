About Me

I am a Ph.D student in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Notre Dame, advised by Dr. Ronald Metoyer. My research lies at the intersection of Human Computer Interaction, Marginalized Populations, Information Retrieval, and Explainable AI.

I leverage my background in Human Computer Interaction to research technology solutions that improve the health and wellbeing of underserved and marginalized communities. Primarily, I am interested in topics related to improving access to social care. I focus on designing systems that enhance coordination and shared understanding for experts who provide social care services.

I came to Notre Dame after obtaining a MS degree in Computer Science from Boise State University (BSU), where I worked with mentors and colleagues in the People and Information Research team. During my program at BSU, I designed interfaces and implemented algorithms centered on enhancing children's experiences when utilizing search engines.

I am passionate about promoting diversity in CS and engaging in activities to teach underrepresented groups, especially females and children, about coding. If you are looking for someone to speak at your event, help organize, or serve as a mentor, please contact me via email or LinkedIn.
