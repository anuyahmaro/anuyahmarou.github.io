Information Retrieval for Kids

Publications

Popular search engines are primarily designed for a general audience who is already good at using them. This makes it challenging for young children to properly utilize these tools. Some of the struggles children face when using propular search engines pertains to query formulation, accessing child-friendly web resources, and search intent interpretation. Motivated by these issues, our research goal was to improve the resources presented to children so they better match their experience and vocabulary. We designed and developed software modules that can interpret a child’s search intent, offer child friendly query suggestions, and provide suitable web results in response to queries written by children.
